What to write for your first email

Your first email doesn't need to be complicated. The goal is a simple, genuine message from your business.

Tips for a good first email
1
Keep it short
2–4 short paragraphs is ideal. People skim. Get to the point quickly and make it easy to read on a phone.
2
Write from a person, not a company
Use "I" or "we" rather than your business name. Personalize your sign-off with your name and role. It builds trust.
3
Have one clear purpose
A welcome note, a seasonal update, a new offering, a simple thank-you. Pick one thing and say it well. Don't try to cover everything.
4
Include one clear next step
A call, a booking link, a reply — give readers one thing to do. Multiple CTAs split attention and reduce action.
