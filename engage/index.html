Email Warming — Design Review

Three new UI surfaces to protect new senders from damaging their domain reputation on their first broadcast.

Design in review
Designs

First Send Onboarding (FTUE)
Full-page walkthrough shown on first Engage session. Explains domain warming, recommends the Engaged Customers segment, and teaches what to write for a good first email.

Campaign Paused Banner
UI shown when the circuit breaker auto-pauses an in-flight send. Includes a global Engage-level alert, a flagged row in the broadcasts list, and a detail banner with actions to fix the audience.
Global alert List indicator Detail banner Sprint 1
Background
Problem: New Engage tenants blast their full customer list on day one with an unwarmed domain, damaging sender reputation and causing future emails to land in spam.

Approach: Three layered defenses — a backend circuit breaker (Sprint 1) that auto-pauses sends when bounce/complaint thresholds are hit, inline warming guidance in the broadcast builder (Sprint 2), and a first-run onboarding flow (Sprint 3) that orients new senders before they ever hit send.

Thresholds: Pause at ≥2% hard bounce rate or ≥0.1% complaint rate (Gmail 2024 mandate).
